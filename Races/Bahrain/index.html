Bahrain International Circuit

First Grand Prix:2004

Laps: 57

Circuit Length: 5.412 km

Race Distance: 308.238 km

Lap Record: 1:31.447 (Pedro de la Rosa, McLaren, 2005)

Brief Info:

  • Location: Sakhir, Bahrain
  • Constructed: Ground broken in December 2002, designed by Hermann Tilke
  • Debut: Inaugural race in 2004, won by Michael Schumacher
  • Circuit Highlights: Exciting races, overtaking opportunities, and technical challenges
  • Attractions: Stunning resorts, beautiful beaches, and the Bahrain International Karting Circuit
  • Best Viewing Spots:
    1. Batelco Grandstand: Offers a great view of cars charging into Turn 8, hammering through 9 and 10, and then passing you on the run down to the short-apex Turn 11.
    2. Outside Grandstand Turns 1 and 2: Allows you to watch overtaking aplenty on either track layout.
